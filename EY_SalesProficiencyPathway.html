The Mandate

Why a Proficiency Pathway?

A curriculum tells people what to attend. A Proficiency Pathway defines what they must demonstrate. That shift moves EY from measuring participation to measuring demonstrated capability.

The Foundation

Built on EY's Investment

EY's Brilliant Basics program, defined sales certification tracks, and AMF pillars provide the foundation. The Proficiency Pathway extends this into a full career development framework.

The Roles

Who This Is For

Account Managers, Account Executives, Sales Executives across all pillars. Long-term vision: Senior Managers, Directors, Partners globally.

Current Situation

  • EY has depth of L&D content including Brilliant Basics, but high variability in sales execution across the organization.
  • Different geo regions impart training differently, resulting in wide range of skill levels that are difficult to compare.
  • Many leadership team members are new, bringing their own methods and creating inconsistency.
  • Past attempts to build a certification program were not well received, creating apathy for testing.
  • Nearly 50% of the workforce are new joiners — loss of certification momentum.

Key Needs

  • Assess competencies and skill levels required by different sales roles for consistent sales culture and process.
  • Develop consistency of language, method, and practices through a combination of learning methods and field execution.
  • Devise a non-academic accreditation methodology to assess skills gained by employees.
  • Develop a Change Management approach demonstrating program benefits to the organization.
  • Help pilot the program in EY's North American organization and scale it further at a later date.

The Problem

  • Significant variability in sales execution across a globally distributed organization
  • Loss of certification momentum; nearly 50% of the workforce are new joiners
  • Inconsistent adoption of sales process, tools, and language across markets
  • No defined path for sellers to build and demonstrate capability

The Solution

  • A role-calibrated competency model defining required skills and behaviors by role
  • Proficiency standards that define performance expectations at each level
  • A framework for role-specific learning pathways, mapping existing curriculum to development needs

Current — Curriculum-Based

  • Complete the module, earn the badge
  • Skills assumed from course completion
  • Knowledge tested by quiz scores
  • Development happens off the desk, away from real work
  • Forgotten within 48 hours without reinforcement
  • Same badge whether or not behavior changed
  • Completion rate is the measure of success

Recommended — Capability-Based

  • Skills demonstrated on live accounts and pursuits
  • Evidence reviewed by a coach decides standing
  • Effort measured by applied behavior, not attendance
  • Development embedded in the rhythm of business
  • Reinforced at increasing intervals until it sticks
  • Standing earned only when behavior is observable
  • Behavior change is the measure of success

Spaced Repetition

Knowledge prompts resurface at Day 1, Day 7, and Day 30. Retention builds through repeated retrieval, not a single pass.

Embedded Practice

Every skill is applied on a real account or pursuit. No classroom simulations substitute for live client work.

Cohort Accountability

Peers and managers see the same evidence. Learning is social and observed, not private and self-reported.

Coach-Certified Standing

A named coach reviews work products and certifies readiness. Advancement requires human judgment, not a quiz score.

Program Structure & Rhythm
How the program runs — embedded in the natural rhythm of the business

A Self-Assessment

Skill-by-skill checklist against behaviors defined for the role and level. Individual notes evidence, flags where they need support. Becomes the working document for the manager alignment conversation.

A Six-Month Cadence

Formal structured review with the leader twice a year. Three inputs required: self-assessment, manager alignment, KPI thresholds. All three must clear — no single input is sufficient.

An Ongoing Rhythm

Development runs through existing meetings: pipeline calls, account reviews, 1:1s. Manager and coach provide informal feedback between formal gates. Curriculum supports the journey; the job is where capability builds.

Connected to Talent Reviews

Standing feeds into talent reviews and bench strength conversations. Succession slates for larger accounts and priority pursuits require demonstrated standing. Gives talent reviews a consistent, evidence-based input beyond manager opinion.

Success Metrics — Measuring Behavior Change, Not Course Completion

Headline metric: share of revenue won by teams at or above Practitioner standing, closing without partner rescue.

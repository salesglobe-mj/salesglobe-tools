The Opportunity
Onebrief has grown from ~60 to 400+ people in two years. The compensation architecture needs to scale to match — ready for the FY2027 (October 1) planning cycle.

Where Onebrief Is Today

  • 400+ employees, ~130 incentive-eligible roles across five distinct populations
  • Government fiscal year (ends Sept 30) with sales concentrated in a 90-day July–September window
  • No clean split between revenue-outcome and customer-health roles — overlap and "turf wars"
  • Enterprise deal sizes $2M–$50M+ — a single win can satisfy a full-year quota

What This Engagement Delivers

  • Five purpose-built incentive plans — differentiated mechanics per population
  • A capture incentive framework for a pre-sales, multi-year government-shaping function
  • A quota methodology built for large-deal, government-fiscal-year economics
  • A final plan architecture the incoming CRO can inherit with confidence — ready for FY2027
These are plans built for a startup. Onebrief now needs a design built for a 400-person, government-fiscal-year business.
A fundamental redesign — not another annual iteration — grounded in analysis of what you pay for today and calibrated to each role's proximity to revenue.

Challenges & Objectives
What we heard from Onebrief, and what this initiative is designed to achieve.
What We Heard
Objectives

Plans built for a startup, not a scale-up

Two years ago Onebrief had ~60 employees. Now at 400 people and ~130 incentive-eligible roles across five distinct populations, the comp architecture needs a fundamental redesign — not another annual iteration.

Government fiscal year creates structural distortion

Onebrief follows the federal fiscal year (ends Sept 30), with the majority of annual sales closing in a concentrated 90-day window (July–September). Standard SaaS commission mechanics don't map to this model.

Role confusion between sales and customer relations

No clean accountability split between teams managing revenue outcomes and those managing customer health — creating overlap, comp inequity, and "turf wars" that misalign incentives.

Capture organization has no incentive framework

A small but strategically critical team shapes government opportunities 2–3 years before they exist as bids — a function that can't be measured on annual quota attainment and has no purpose-built incentive design.

Quota architecture doesn't match deal economics

Deal sizes range from ~$2M to $50M+, creating a dynamic where a single enterprise win can satisfy a seller's full-year quota — skewing behavior, distorting attainment distributions, and limiting the plan's ability to drive consistent full-year performance.

Design five purpose-built plans

Differentiated mechanics for capture, sales (net new), account management, customer/mission success, and ops delivery.

Resolve government fiscal-year mechanics

A comp architecture that accounts for the July–September revenue concentration, structural quota distortion, and unique deal economics of the Onebrief model.

Build a capture incentive framework

Purpose-built incentives that reflect the capture team's pre-sales government development role and multi-year deal horizons.

Address quota methodology for large deals

Redesign the quota-setting approach for enterprise deal-size variability and to motivate consistent full-year performance.

Deliver a final plan architecture ready for FY2027

A complete, defensible design ready for the October 1 rollout and the incoming CRO to inherit.
